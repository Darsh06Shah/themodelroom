# The Model Room — Interview Defense Notes
How to actually *say* the answers, not just read them. The differentiator that gets offers.

## Three statements (the $10 depreciation walk)
Start with the income statement, not the balance sheet. "Net income drops $7.50 because of the tax
shield, then on the cash flow statement I add depreciation back, so cash only falls $2.50, and the
balance sheet ties because PP&E and retained earnings fall by the same net amount." Interviewers
watch for whether you remember the cash tax effect. Most forget it.

## LBO in 30 seconds
"Buy at an entry multiple on EBITDA, fund it with debt and equity, pay down debt with free cash
flow, exit at year 5 at the same or a better multiple. Return comes from debt paydown, EBITDA
growth, and multiple expansion. MOIC is exit equity over entry equity; IRR is the annualized rate."
Say "same multiple" first. Do not assume multiple expansion unless they ask.

## Valuation: which method, when
DCF for stable cash flows and when you control assumptions; comparables for a quick market read;
precedents for control transactions (they include a premium). If they ask which is highest, say
precedents usually, but it depends on the cycle.

## "Tell me about a deal you found interesting"
Pick ONE live deal in your target bank's sector. Know: acquirer, target, size, stock vs cash, and
why it makes strategic sense. 30 seconds. Confidence beats complexity.

## Behavioral: why this bank
Name a real recent deal or the bank's model (e.g. advisory-only at Evercore means no balance-sheet
conflicts). Generic "prestige" answers lose. Specificity wins.
