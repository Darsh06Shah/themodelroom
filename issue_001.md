## The one theme
2026 is the year of the **megadeal**. M&A volume is softer in count but boards are chasing fewer, bigger transactions. If you walk into an interview and say "M&A is slow," you will sound behind. Say "megadeal volume is back, count is down" and you sound like you read the tape.

## 5 deals to know
1. **BlackRock (GIP) & EQT acquire AES Corporation — $33.4bn.** Announced March 2026. Biggest one so far. Infrastructure + energy. Advisor names will be all over this.
2. **KKR-led consortium buys Australia's Steadfast — $5.3bn.** July 14, 2026. PE loves insurance-distribution roll-ups right now.
3. **US public M&A H1 2026** was defined by "transactions of exceptional scale" per White & Case — deal volume softened but size grew.
4. **Barclays' 2026 M&A outlook:** fewer but bolder strategic mergers; acceleration expected through the year.
5. **Chevron–Hess, Nippon–US Steel, Alphabet–Wiz** — still the reference deals recruiters cite when they ask "what deal are you watching."

## 3 charts to be able to describe
- **Megadeal count vs. total value:** value up, count flat. The "barbell" market.
- **PE deal pace:** insurance and infra distribution are the hot sub-sectors (see Steadfast).
- **Rate path vs. exit multiples:** lower expected rates = higher EV/EBITDA. Be ready to explain why a DCF and a comps approach diverge right now.

## The recruiting angle (why this matters to you)
- Goldman's summer acceptance rate is around **1.16%** (250k applications, ~2,900 spots). You need an edge that is not "I'm hardworking."
- The edge is **specificity.** When they ask "a deal you found interesting," name one from above and know acquirer, size, structure (stock/cash), and the strategic logic. 30 seconds.
