Question of the week

Pulled from real interviews. (Full bank in the vault — firm-tagged.)
"A $10 increase in depreciation — walk me through the impact on all three statements."
Answer: IS — pre-tax income −$10, net income −$7.50 (at 25%). Cash Flow — +$10 add-back → net cash +$2.50. Balance Sheet — cash +$2.50, PP&E (net) −$10, retained earnings −$7.50. Both sides fall $7.50. It balances. (This is the single most-asked technical — and most people miss the second-order cash tax effect.)
