Question of the week

Pulled from real interviews. The full bank is in the vault, tagged by firm.
"A $10 increase in depreciation. Walk me through the impact on all three statements."
Answer: Income statement, pre-tax income falls $10, net income falls $7.50 at a 25% rate. Cash flow, add back the $10 depreciation, so net cash up $2.50. Balance sheet, cash up $2.50, PP&E (net) down $10, retained earnings down $7.50. Both sides drop $7.50. It balances. This is the most-asked technical. Most people miss the second-order cash tax effect.
